Blogs



  • RISK FACTOR

    Genetics: Family history of ADHD increases the likelihood of a child developing the disorder. Genetic factors contribute to differences in brain function and structure associated with ADHD. Environmental factors also play a role in the disorder's development.

    Read More

  • DIAGNOSIS

    Clinical Assessment: ADHD diagnosis relies on clinical assessment by healthcare professionals. It involves gathering information from various sources to evaluate symptoms against diagnostic criteria outlined in the DSM-5. Other conditions with similar symptoms are ruled out to ensure accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.

    Read More

  • TREATMENT

    Stimulant Medications: Commonly prescribed for ADHD treatment, stimulant medications like methylphenidate and amphetamines increase neurotransmitter levels, improving attention and impulse control. However, they're often part of a broader treatment plan that may include behavioral therapy and educational interventions.
